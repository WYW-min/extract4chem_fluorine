78: # FTIR Studies
79: 
80: Figure 3 shows the FTIR spectra of acid and amino functionalized MWCNTs. In the acid functionalized MWCNTs, the peaks at 1634 and  $1295~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$  correspond to  $\mathrm{C = O}$  and C-O stretching, respectively. The two weak peaks at 2917 and  $2847~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$  correspond to the -CH stretching mode. A broad peak corresponding to strong absorption at 3433 and  $3150~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$  can be ascribed to -OH stretching vibration in -COOH group. In the amino functionalized MWCNTs, a strong and broad peak at  $1631~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$  with a shoulder at  $1707~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$  are ascribed to  $\mathrm{C = O}$  stretching vibration due to the formation of amide linkage. 87: For comparison, the FTIR spectra of the neat polyimide film is shown in Fig. 4a. A strong and broad peak at  $1395~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$  and another at  $3556~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$  are ascribed to the C-N stretching of imide ring. Characteristic strong peak due to  $\mathrm{C = O}$  stretching appears at  $1720~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$ , while asymmetric stretching of  $\mathrm{C = O}$  as a weak peak at  $1777~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$ . The peak at  $725~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$  corresponds to bending vibration of  $\mathrm{C = O}$  group. C-C stretching of the aromatic ring appears at  $1515~\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$ . The studies confirm the formation of polyimide and match well with the earlier reported data [14].
