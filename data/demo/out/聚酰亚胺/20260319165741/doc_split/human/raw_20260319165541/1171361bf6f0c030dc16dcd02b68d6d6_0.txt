49: Abstract: A benzimidazole-containing diamine monomer was prepared via a simple one-step synthesis process. A two-step procedure involving polycondensation in the presence of aromatic dianhydrides  $(4,4^{\prime}$  -oxydiphthalic anhydride, ODPA) followed by thermal imidization was then performed to prepare a benzimidazole-based polyimide (BI-PI). BI-PI membranes were fabricated using an electrospinning technique and were hot pressed for  $30\mathrm{min}$  at  $200^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  under a pressure of  $50\mathrm{kgf / cm}^2$  Finally, the hot-pressed membranes were assembled into supercapacitors, utilizing high-porosityactivated water chestnut shell biochar as the active material. The TGA results showed that the BI-PI polymer produced in the two-step synthesis process had a high thermal stability  $\left(\mathrm{T_{d}5\% = 527^{\circ}C}\right)$  Moreover, the hot-press process reduced the pore size in the BI-PI membrane and improved the pore-size uniformity. The hot-press procedure additionally improved the mechanical properties of the BI-PI membrane, resulting in a high tensile modulus of  $783\mathrm{MPa}$  and a tensile strength of  $34.8\mathrm{MPa}$  The cyclic voltammetry test results showed that the membrane had a specific capacitance of  $121\mathrm{F / g}$  and a capacitance retention of  $77\%$  . By contrast, a commercial cellulose separator showed a specific capacitance value of  $107\mathrm{F / g}$  and a capacitance retention of  $49\%$  under the same scanning conditions. Finally, the membrane showed both a small equivalent series resistance  $(\mathbb{R}_s)$  and a small interfacial resistance  $(\mathbb{R}_{\mathrm{ct}})$  . Overall, the results showed that the BI-PI membrane has significant potential as a separator for high-performance supercapacitor applications.
