198: # 4. Conclusion
199: 
200: Novel lightweight mechanically resistant composites have been fabricated using glass fiber as a 3D reinforcement for PI aerogels to produce materials with a porous polymeric matrix. Conventional expensive chemical cross-linking agents have been replaced with porous glass fiber which acts as a skeleton for the aerogel to suppress collapse and shrinkage during the composite preparation process. The resultant GF/PI composites exhibit low density (0.143 to  $0.177\mathrm{gcm}^{-3}$ ) and high compressive strengths, as well as excellent thermal stability and low thermal conductivity (0.023 to  $0.029\mathrm{Wm}^{-1}\mathrm{K}^{-1}$  at room temperature and 0.057 to  $0.082\mathrm{Wm}^{-1}\mathrm{K}^{-1}$  at  $500^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ) values. These properties are highly promising for the development of lightweight and high-mechanical-strength PI composites for use in high temperature insulation applications.
