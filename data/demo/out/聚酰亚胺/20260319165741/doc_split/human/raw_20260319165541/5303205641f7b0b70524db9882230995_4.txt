211: # 4. Conclusions
212: 
213: In summary, a double-gel system polyimide aerogel material was discussed in this study. A  $\mathrm{SiO}_2@GO-NH_2$  amino-modified wet gel was first fabricated via a novel sol-gel method, to obtain a  $\mathrm{SiO}_2$  uniformly coated GO material. Then, the PAA gel and  $\mathrm{SiO}_2@GO-NH_2$  gel were homogeneously dispersed under the liquid phase, to provide the double-gel system. After a series of subsequent treatments, the prepared PI aerogel had a microscopic porous honeycomb structure and multiple-layer interface, which provided good thermal resistance. The strong compatibility and binding of  $\mathrm{SiO}_2@GO-NH_2$  with PI also improved the low density  $(0.0442\mathrm{g/cm}^3)$ , high specific modulus  $(46.19\mathrm{m}^2/\mathrm{s}^2)$ , effective thermal insulation properties  $(0.0309\mathrm{W/m}\cdot\mathrm{K})$ , high thermal stability  $(543.80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  in Ar atmosphere), high hydrophobicity  $(131.55^{\circ})$ , and ability to maintain performance after prolonged exposure to thermal environments. The performance improvement was due to the scientific introduction of  $\mathrm{SiO}_2@GO-NH_2$  and the construction of the double-gel system. The easy preparation process and superior performance make it suitable for wide applications within various industrial and research fields requiring thermal insulation.
