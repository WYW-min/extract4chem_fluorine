247: # 4. Conclusions
248: 
249: In our study, rigid PI foams with different densities were prepared by thermal foaming of a nadimide end-capped polyimide precursor with an  $\alpha$ -BPDA-PDA/BIA structure. The molar ratio of BIA/PDA and the  $\mathrm{M_w}$  of the precursor were found to be two effective factors regulating the performance of PI foams. The prepared PI foams showed excellent combined properties, in which the initial thermal decomposition temperature was  $\geq 490^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  and the Tg was  $395.0^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . PIF-2-1500  $(300\mathrm{kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3})$  showed outstanding mechanical properties with a compressive strength of  $11\mathrm{MPa}$  and a compressive modulus of  $215\mathrm{MPa}$ . Moreover, the foams with a density of  $\geq 250\mathrm{kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}$  were suitable as foam core materials of carbon fiber reinforced polyimide sandwich composites co-cured at  $320^{\circ}\mathrm{C} / 0.4\mathrm{MPa}$ .
