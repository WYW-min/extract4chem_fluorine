102: The porous PI/PVdF composite separator, which was composed of nanofibers with a multicore-shell (MCS) structure, was prepared using a facile electrospinning technique with a single nozzle. The intrinsic immiscibility of polymers allowed for the formation of a novel MCS membrane despite single-nozzle electrospinning. The unique MCS separator showed better thermal stability, mechanical properties, and long-term battery performance than a commercial PE separator due to its well-distributed thermally stable PI nanofibrils in a PVdF shell and external PVdF and electrolyte interactions. Therefore, the MCS separator prepared using single-nozzle electrospinning is a promising material to be used as a thermally stable and active separator for Li-based batteries.
