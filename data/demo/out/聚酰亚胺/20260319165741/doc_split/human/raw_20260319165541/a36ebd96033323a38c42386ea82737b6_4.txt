148: # 4. Conclusions
149: 
150: In this paper, we have demonstrated the PI-silica and GPMS-modified silica composites. Based on the results, the following factors are crucial for developing compatibilized composites:
151: 
152: (i) The substitution of TEOS with GPMS can reduce the formation of large silica particles by using a large number of hydroxyl groups and can also form H-bonds with anhydride, carbonyl, carboxyl, and terminal amine groups in the polyimides/polyamic acids.  
153: (ii) The organic segment of GPMS can contribute significantly to bringing compatibilization between the two phases.  
154: (iii) Epoxy group of GPMS will contribute to the formation of H-bonds or networks with carboxyl acid groups or terminal amine groups of the PAA for the compatibilized composites (homopolymerization and their impacts on the PI-silica composite cannot be ruled out for this kind of system).  
155: (iv) SEM images suggested that the size of the silica particles in the PI/TSi composites was remarkably decreased by the addition of GPMS. Overall, it could be suggested that the incorporation of GPMS can produce compatibilized PI-silica composites with improved optical transmittance and controlled dielectric constants and all of the reported composites have shown  $T_{\mathrm{d5}}$  more than  $460^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . The above investigations will help increase the feasibility of using PI-silica composites in many useful structural applications such as in high-temperature manufacturing plants, electronic packaging, aerospace, and automotive industries.
