136: # 3.1.Materials
137: 
138: The aromatic dianhydride,  $4,4^{\prime}$  (hexafluoroisopropylidene)diphthalic anhydride (6FDA) with a polymerization-grade purity  $(\geq 99.5\%)$  was purchased from ChinaTech (Tianjin) Chem. Co. Ltd. (Tianjin, China), and dried at  $180^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  in vacuo for  $24\mathrm{h}$  prior to use. The fluorocontaining diamines, including 3,5-diaminobenzotrifluoride (TFMDA),  $2^{\prime}$ -trifluoromethyl-3,4'-oxydianiline (3FODA), 1,4-bis[(4-amino-2-trifluoro-methyl)phenoxy]benzene (6FAPB), and  $4,4^{\prime}$ -bis[(4-amino-2-trifluoromethyl)phenoxy]-biphenyl (6FBAB) were purchased from Changzhou Sunlight Pharmaceutical Co., Ltd. (Changzhou, Jiangsu, China) and used directly. Electronic grade  $N$ -methyl-2-pyrrolidinone (NMP) and  $N,N$ -dimethylacetamide (DMAc) were purchased from Greenda Chem. Co. Ltd., China (Hangzhou, Zhejiang, China) and used as received. The other reagents and materials were purchased from Sinopharm Chem. Reagent Co. Ltd. (Shanghai, China) and used as received.

140: # 3.2. Measurements
141: 
142: Inherent viscosity was measured using an Ubbelohde viscometer (Pingxuan Scientific Instrument Co. Ltd., Shanghai, China) with a  $0.5\mathrm{g / dL}$  NMP solution at  $25^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . Attenuated total reflectance-Fourier transform infrared (ATR-FTIR) spectrum was obtained on an Iraffinity-1S FT-IR spectrometer (Shimadzu, Kyoto, Japan).  ${}^{1}\mathrm{H}$ -NMR was performed on an AV 400 spectrometer (Tokyo, Japan), operating at  $300\mathrm{MHz}$  and using DMSO- $\mathrm{d}_6$  as the solvent and tetramethylsilane as the reference. Ultraviolet-visible (UV-Vis) spectra of the PI UFMs were recorded on a Hitachi U-3210 spectrophotometer (Tokyo, Japan) at room temperature with a reflectance mode. The number average molecular weight  $(\mathbf{M}_{\mathfrak{n}})$  and weight average molecular weight  $(\mathbf{M}_{\mathbf{w}})$  of the PI resins were measured using a gel permeation chromatography (GPC) system (Shimadzu, Kyoto, Japan) with a Shodex KF804 column (Tokyo, Japan). HPLC grade NMP was used as the mobile phase at a flow rate of  $1.0~\mathrm{mL / min}$ . Polystyrene (Shodex, Type: SM-105, Showa Denko Co. Ltd., Tokyo, Japan) was used as the standard. Wide-angle X-ray diffraction (XRD) was conducted on a D/max-2500 X-ray diffractometer (Rigaku, Tokyo, Japan). The micro-morphologies of the PI UFMs were detected via a JSM-6700F (JEOL, Tokyo, Japan) field emission scanning electron microscopy (FE-SEM). The color parameters of the PI UFMs were measured using an X-rite color i7 spectrophotometer (Grand Rapids, MI, USA) and calculated according to a CIE (Comission Internationale del'Eclairage) Lab equation.  $\mathbf{L}^{*}$  is the lightness, where 100 means white and 0 implies black. A positive  $\mathbf{a}^{*}$  means a red color, and a negative one indicates a green color. A positive  $\mathbf{b}^{*}$  means a yellow color, and a negative one indicates a blue color. The whiteness indices (WI) of the PI UFMs were calculated as follows:  $\mathrm{WI} = 100 - [(100 - \mathrm{L}^{*})^{2} + \mathrm{a}^{*2} + \mathrm{b}^{*2}]^{1 / 2}$ , where WI standards for whiteness index,  $\mathbf{L}^{*}$  standards for lightness,  $\mathbf{a}^{*}$  and  $\mathbf{b}^{*}$  stand for chromaticity coefficient. The water contact angles (WCAs) of the PI UFMs were measured using the sessile drop method using an OCA-40Micro (Stuttgart, Germany) contact angle analyzer. Five samples were measured and the average WCA values were then obtained. A drop of  $5\mu \mathrm{L}$  deionized water was used for the measurements. Thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) was performed on a TA-Q50 thermal analysis system (New Castle, DE, USA) at a heating rate of  $20^{\circ}\mathrm{Cmin}^{-1}$  in nitrogen. Differential scanning calorimetry (DSC) was carried on a TA-Q 100 thermal analysis system (New Castle, DE, USA) at a heating rate of  $10^{\circ}\mathrm{Cmin}^{-1}$  in nitrogen.
143: 
144: Solubility of the PI resins in various solvents was determined by adding  $1.0\mathrm{g}$  of the PI resin into  $9.0\mathrm{g}$  of the tested solvent at room temperature (10 wt% solid content). The mixture was mechanically stirred at  $25^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $24\mathrm{h}$ . The solubility was determined visually as three grades: completely soluble  $(++)$ , partially soluble  $(+)$ , and insoluble  $(-)$ . The complete solubility is defined as a homogenous and clean solution is obtained, in which no phase separation, precipitation or gel formation is detected.

146: # 3.3. Synthesis of  $4^{\prime}$ -tert-butyl-cyclohexyl-3,5-diaminobenzoate (DABC)
147: 
148: DABC was synthesized in our laboratory with 3,5-dinitrobenzyl chloride and  $4^{\prime}$ -tert-butylcyclohexyol as the starting materials, according to the similar procedure reported in the literature, and purified by recrystallization from absolute ethanol [38]. DABC was obtained as white needles with a purity of  $99.5\%$  according to the gas chromatography analysis. Yield:  $57\%$ . Melting point:  $192.0^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  (DSC peak temperature). FT-IR  $(\mathrm{KBr}, \mathrm{cm}^{-1})$ : 3472, 3441, 3341, 3210, 2951, 2862, 1694, 1597, 1458, 1366, 1254, 1196, and 775.  ${}^{1}\mathrm{H}$ -NMR (DMSO- $d_6$ , ppm): 6.41–6.34 (m, 2H), 6.01–5.97 (m, 1H), 4.95–4.89 (m, 4H), 4.69–4.61 (m, 1H), 2.04–1.96 (m, 2H), 1.81–1.73 (m, 2H), 1.36–1.30 (m, 2H), 1.14–0.98 (m, 3H), and 0.85–0.79 (m, 9H).  ${}^{13}\mathrm{C}$ -NMR (DMSO- $d_6$ , ppm): 166.7, 149.7, 131.7, 104.1, 73.5, 46.9, 32.5, 32.3, 27.9, and 25.6. Elemental analysis: calculated for  $\mathrm{C_{17}H_{26}N_2O_2}$  ( $\mathrm{M_w}$ : 290.40 g/mol): C, 70.31%, H, 9.02%, N, 9.65%; Found: C, 70.37%, H, 9.05%, N, 9.55%.

150: # 3.4. Preparation of PI Resins and Electrospun UFMs
151: 
152: PI resins were prepared from 6FDA and the diamines via a two-step chemical imidization procedure, which could be illustrated by the synthesis of FPI-5 (6FDA-DABC). DABC  $(14.5200\mathrm{g},50\mathrm{mmol})$  and DMAc  $(100.0\mathrm{g})$  were filled into a three-necked  $500~\mathrm{mL}$  flask equipped with a mechanical stirrer, a nitrogen inlet, and a cold-water bath. The diamine solution was stirred at  $10 - 15^{\circ}C$  for  $30\mathrm{min}$  under nitrogen and 6FDA  $(22.2120\mathrm{g},50\mathrm{mmol})$  was added in one batch together with an additional DMAc  $(10.2\mathrm{g})$ . The solid content of the polymerization mixture was controlled to be  $25\mathrm{wt}\%$ . The cold bath was removed after  $4\mathrm{h}$  and the viscous solution was further stirred at room temperature for another  $20\mathrm{h}$ . A pale-yellow poly(amic acid) (PAA) solution was obtained. Then, acetic anhydride  $(30.6\mathrm{g},300\mathrm{mmol})$  and pyridine  $(19.0\mathrm{g},240\mathrm{mmol})$  were added into the PAA solution with vigorous stirring. The chemical imidization procedure was performed at room temperature for another  $24\mathrm{h}$ . At last, the polymerization mixture was slowly poured into an excess of aqueous ethanol solution  $(75\mathrm{wt}\%)$ . FPI-5 resin was then precipitated from the PI solution. The resin was thoroughly immersed into the ethanol solution and then collected. The wet resin was dried under vacuum at  $120^{\circ}C$  for  $24\mathrm{h}$  to afford the FPI-5 resin as a pale-yellow fibrous resin. Yield:  $34.06\mathrm{g}(97.5\%)$ .
153: 
154: The other PI resins, including FPI-1 (6FDA-TFMDA), FPI-2 (6FDA-3FODA), FPI-3 (6FDA-6FAPB), and FPI-4 (6FDA-6FBAB) were prepared according to a similar procedure as mentioned above, except DABC was replaced by TFMDA for FPI-1, by 3FODA for FPI-2, by 6FAPB for FPI-3, and by 6FBAB for FPI-4, respectively.

156: # 3.5. Electrospinning Fabrication
157: 
158: The FPI UFMs were fabricated via a one-step electrospinning procedure reported in the literature [39]. FPI-5 UFM was used as an example to present the detailed procedure. Fully dried FPI-5 resin was dissolved into ultra-dry DMAc at a solid content of  $15\mathrm{wt}\%$ . The obtained FPI-5 solution was filtered through a  $0.45\mu \mathrm{m}$  Teflon filtration membrane to remove any undissolved impurities. Then, the FPI-5 solution was filled into a  $5\mathrm{mL}$  high-density polyethylene syringe equipped with a spinneret with an inner diameter of  $0.21\mathrm{mm}$ . A syringe pump was used to squeeze out the PI solution through the needle spinneret at a speed of  $0.2\mathrm{mL/h}$ . A voltage of  $15\mathrm{kV}$  was applied between the syringe and the collector. The distance between the spinneret and the grounded plate collector was  $15\mathrm{cm}$ . The collector was the aluminum foil covered onto the rotating drum (diameter:  $10\mathrm{cm}$ ; length:  $30\mathrm{cm}$ ; speed:  $200\mathrm{rpm}$ ). The relative humidity in the electrospinning apparatus was controlled to be  $35\pm 5\%$ . The FPI-5 fibers were continuously deposited onto the aluminum foil and formed the final FPI-5 UFM. After the electrospinning fabrication, the FPI-5 NFM was placed in a circulating oven at  $200^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\mathrm{h}$  to remove the residual solvent.
159: 
160: The other FPI UFMs, including FPI-1 (6FDA-TFMDA), FPI-2 (6FDA-3FODA), FPI-3 (6FDA-6FAPB), and FPI-4 (6FDA-6FBAB), were prepared according to a similar procedure. The PI-ref UFM was made by a two-step procedure according to the literature [25]. PAA UFM was first prepared and then thermally dehydrated at  $300^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  to afford the PI-ref UFM.
