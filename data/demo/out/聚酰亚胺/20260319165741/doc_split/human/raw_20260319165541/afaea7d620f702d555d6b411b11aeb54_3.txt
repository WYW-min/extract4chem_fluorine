31: # 2.1. Materials
32: 
33: 4,4'-Isopropylidenediphenoxy-bis(phthalic anhydride) (6HDA), and 4,4'- diaminodiphenylmethane (DDM) were purchased from Aldrich, and has been used as received. Melting point (m.p.) of 6HDA was  $184 - 187^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ; m. p. of DDM was  $90 - 92^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . 3,3'-Dimethyl-4,4'-diaminodiphenylmethane (MMDA) was obtained in our laboratory by a previously reported method [10,11]. m.p of MMDA was  $155 - 157^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ .
34: 
35: # 2.2. Polymer synthesis
36: 
37: The investigated polymers have been obtained by two-step polycondensation reaction [12]. The first step was performed at room temperature with equimolar amounts of one aromatic diamine (DDM or MMDA) and dianhydride (6HDA) in N,N-dimethylacetamide (DMAc), at a total concentration of  $14-15\%$ , under inert atmosphere during  $6 - 7\mathrm{h}$ . In the second step, thermal imidization of the resulting poly(amic acid) in solution was performed in the same reaction flask by heating the solution at reflux temperature  $(170 - 180^{\circ}\mathrm{C})$  for  $5 - 6\mathrm{h}$ , under a slow stream of nitrogen to remove the water of imidization. The final product was precipitated in water, washed with water and ethanol, and dried in vacuum oven at  $105^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . The structures of the resulted polyimides are shown in Figure 1.

45: # 2.3. Preparation of films
46: 
47: The polyimide films were obtained by casting the polyimide solutions of  $15\%$  concentration in DMAc onto a glass plate and by drying it at  $60^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $4\mathrm{h}$ . Then the films were further dried at  $100^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , 150, 200, and  $250^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\mathrm{h}$  at each temperature. Finally the films were kept at  $260^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $2\mathrm{h}$  and they were used afterwards for various measurements. The thickness of such films was in the range of  $30 - 40 \mu \mathrm{m}$ . In polyimide films, we have to consider interactions with electronic charge transfer between polymer chains. Polymer chains resulting from load transfer interactions form nano-aggregates in which pairs of atomic structures appear as electronic charge donors and pairs as electronic load acceptors (Figure 2).

56: # 2.4. Ni and Cu nanoparticle deposition
57: 
58: The metal nanoparticles were embedded using the deposition set-up presented in Figure 3. The Cu and Ni targets were mounted on a multi-axis manipulator placed inside the vacuum chamber. The experiments were conducted at a pressure of  $\sim 10^{-2}$  Torr. The polyimide films were placed on the substrate holder at a  $2\mathrm{cm}$  distance from the target surface. The energy of the laser pulse  $(30\mathrm{mJ})$  and the irradiated surface  $(1.5\mathrm{mm}^2)$  were kept constant during all experiments, leading to a fluence of  $2\mathrm{J/cm}^2$ . The varied experimental parameter was the deposition time. Half of the experiments were done by irradiating the target for 40 seconds and half for  $600\mathrm{s}$ . Table 1 summarizes the investigated samples and the conditions in which were obtained.

67: # 2.5. Atomic force microscopy (AFM)
68: 
69: The metal-polymer nano-aggregates were studied by performing the atomic force microscopy(AFM) measurements on a Scanning Probe Microscope (Solver PRO-M, NTMDT, Russia), at room temperature  $(23^{\circ}\mathrm{C})$ , in semi-contact mode, using a commercially available NSG10 (Solver PRO-M, NTMDT, Russia) silicon cantilever with a tetrahedral shape tip with curvature radius of  $10~\mathrm{nm}$ . The resonant frequency of the cantilever was  $373~\mathrm{kHz}$ . The cantilever's planar dimensions were: length  $= 95\pm 5~\mu \mathrm{m}$ , width  $= 30\pm 5~\mu \mathrm{m}$ , thickness  $= 2\pm 0.5~\mu \mathrm{m}$ . For image acquisition on  $5\times 5~\mu \mathrm{m}^2$ , Nova v.1.26.0.1443 software was used. In order to calculate the 3D morphological parameters and to make the grain analysis, Image Analysis 3.5.0.18542 software was used.
70: 
71: # 2.6. Scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and Energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDX)
72: 
73: Images have been acquired through scanning electron microscopy using a Crossbeam System Neon 40ESB FIB/SEM microscope from Carl Zeiss, with thermal Schottky field emission, equipped with EDS, In-lens, SE and EsB detectors. Electron beam resolution:  $1.1\div 2.5$  nm for  $\mathrm{U} = 20\div 1\mathrm{kV}$ . Electrons accelerating voltage used for the SEM images-  $20\mathrm{kV}$ . Working distance was around  $5\mathrm{mm}$ . For the EDX analysis an X-Max 50 from Oxford Instruments detector was used; electrons accelerating voltage  $20\mathrm{kV}$ , working distance  $5.1\mathrm{mm}$ .
