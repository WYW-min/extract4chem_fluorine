65: # Preparation of the organophilic clays BAOB-MMT
66: 
67: Sodium montmorillonite  $(2.000\mathrm{g})$  was stirred in  $100~\mathrm{ml}$  of distilled water (beaker A) at room temperature overnight. A separate solution containing BAOB  $(0.9152\mathrm{g}, 2.308\mathrm{mmol})$  in  $200~\mathrm{ml}$  of distilled water (beaker B) was kept under magnetic stirring at  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  and then  $35\%$  aqueous HCl was added to adjust the pH value to about 3-4. After complete dissolution of diamine and stirring for additional  $1\mathrm{h}$ , the contents of beaker
68: 
69: A were added at approximately  $10\mathrm{ml / min}$  under vigorous stirring and then stirred at  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for an hour. The organoclay was filtered in a Buchner funnel. To remove any excess of ammonium ions, the precipitate was washed with distilled water until a chloride ion test with a  $0.1\mathrm{MAgNO}_3$  solution was negative and then dried in vacuum at  $50^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $48\mathrm{h}$  [47].
