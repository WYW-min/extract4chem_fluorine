100: Imidization of PAA was confirmed by Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) analysis and TGA tests. In the spectrum of PAA nanofiber membrane (Figure 2(a)), characteristic bands were observed at 3250, 3050, 1650, and  $1602\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$ , which were attributed to OH stretching (in carboxyl group), NH stretching (in amide group), OH bending, and NH bending, respectively (Socrates, 2001). These bands were hardly identified in the spectrum of heated counterpart. 109: The TGA and corresponding derivative thermo-gravimetry (DTG) curves of the heated nanofibers are shown in Figure 2(b). All the samples displayed similar TGA and DTG patterns. For the clarity, only the results of sample C3 were showed. The membranes decomposed initially at  $532^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ $(\mathrm{T_{d5\%}})$  and had a highest weight decomposition temperature  $(\mathrm{T_{dmax}})$  as high as  $591^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , similar to the results of pure PI fibers reported in previous work (Çakmakçı and Güngör, 2013; Wang et al., 2016). In addition, merely  $1\%$  of weight loss occurred in the heated samples at  $260^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , implying a relatively high thermal stability of PI nanofibers required for hot gas filtration.
