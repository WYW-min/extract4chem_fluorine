11: # Alicyclic Polyimide/SiO $_2$  Mixed Matrix Membranes for Water/n-Butanol Pervaporation
12: 
13: Ching-Wen Hsieh  $^{1}$ , Bo-Xian Li  $^{1}$  and Shing-Yi Suen  $^{1,2,*}$

19: Abstract: Alicyclic polyimides (PIs) have excellent properties in solubility, mechanical strength, thermal property, etc. This study developed two types of alicyclic PI-based mixed matrix membranes (MMMs) for water/n-butanol pervaporation application, which have never been investigated previously. The fillers were hydrophilic  $\mathrm{SiO}_2$  nanoparticles. The synthesized PI was mixed with  $\mathrm{SiO}_2$  nanoparticles in DMAc to make the casting solution, and a liquid film was formed over PET substrate using doctor blade. A dense MMM was fabricated at  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  and further treated via multistage curing  $(100 - 170^{\circ}\mathrm{C})$ . The prepared membranes were characterized by FTIR, TGA, FE-SEM, water contact angle, and solvent swelling. The trends of pure solvent swelling effects agree well with the water contact angle results. Moreover, the pervaporation efficiencies of alicyclic PI/ $\mathrm{SiO}_2$  MMMs for  $85\mathrm{wt}\%$  n-butanol aqueous solution at  $40^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  were investigated. The results showed that BCDA-3,4'-ODA/ $\mathrm{SiO}_2$  MMMs had a larger permeation flux and higher separation factor than BCDA-1,3,3-APB/ $\mathrm{SiO}_2$  MMMs. For both types of MMMs, the separation factor increased first and then decreased, with increasing  $\mathrm{SiO}_2$  loading. Based on the PSI performance, the optimal  $\mathrm{SiO}_2$  content was  $0.5\mathrm{wt}\%$  for BCDA-3,4'-ODA/ $\mathrm{SiO}_2$  MMMs and  $5\mathrm{wt}\%$  for BCDA-1,3,3-APB/ $\mathrm{SiO}_2$  MMMs. The overall separation efficiency of BCDA-3,4'-ODA-based membranes was 10-30-fold higher.
