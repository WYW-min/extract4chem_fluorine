69: # 2. Materials and Methods
70: 
71: # 2.1. Materials
72: 
73: $4,4^{\prime}$ -Oxydiphthalic anhydride (ODPA, Changzhou Sunshine Pharmaceutical Co., Ltd., Changzhou, China) and 4-methylethynyl phthalic anhydride (MEPA, Nexam Chemical, Lomma, Sweden) were dried at  $180~^\circ \mathrm{C}$  and  $120~^\circ \mathrm{C}$  for  $10\mathrm{h}$ , respectively, prior to use. 3,3-bis[4-(4-aminophenoxy)phenyl]phthalide (BAPPT) was synthesized in our laboratory according to the literature [29]. Electronic-grade  $N,N$ -dimethylacetamide (DMAc),  $N,N$ -dimethylformamide (DMF), and  $N$ -methylpyrrolidone (NMP), purity  $\geq 99.8\%$ ,  $\mathrm{Na^{+}} \leq 2$  ppm, water content  $\leq 200$  ppm (Grinda Electronic Materials Co., Ltd., Hangzhou, China) were used directly.
74: 
75: # 2.2. Characterization and Methods
76: 
77: The inherent viscosity of METI resins was measured at  $25^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  using an Ubbelohde viscometer (As-one, Osaka, Japan), and the absolute viscosity was measured at  $25^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  using a DV2-TRVCP viscometer (Brookfield, Middleboro, MA, USA). Molecular weights of METI resins, including number-average molecular weight  $(\mathbf{M}_{\mathrm{n}})$  and weight-average molecular weight  $(\mathbf{M}_{\mathrm{w}})$ , were determined using an LC-20AD-type gel permeation chromatography (GPC, Shimadzu Corporation, Kyoto, Japan) system. Solubility tests were characterized as follows:  $1.0\mathrm{g}$  of METI resins was mixed with  $9.0\mathrm{g}$  of solvent (solid content  $10\mathrm{wt}\%$ )
78: 
79: and stirred at room temperature for  $24\mathrm{h}$ . Its solubility was visually judged and divided into three grades: completely dissolved  $(++)$ , partially dissolved  $(+ -)$ , and insoluble  $(-)$ , where complete dissolution means that the resin is uniformly dispersed in the solution, transparent, without phase separation, precipitation, or gel formation; insolubilization indicates no change in the appearance of the resin.
80: 
81: Attenuated total reflection-Fourier transform infrared (ATR-FTIR) spectra of PI NFMs were measured by a Bruker Tensor-27 FT-IR spectrometer (Bruker, Billerica, MA, USA), wavenumber range:  $4000 - 400\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$ . Wide-angle X-ray diffraction (XRD) spectra were measured with a D/max-2500 X-ray diffractometer (Rigaku Corporation, Tokyo, Japan), Cu-Kα1 radiation, operating at  $40\mathrm{kV}$  and  $200\mathrm{mA}$ . Field-emission scanning electron microscope (FE-SEM) imaging was performed using a JSM-6700F (JEOL, Tokyo, Japan) with an accelerating voltage of  $15\mathrm{kV}$ , and Pt/Pd was sputtered on each film before measurement. Ultraviolet-visible spectroscopy (UV-Vis) was performed at room temperature using a U-3210 spectrophotometer (Hitachi, Tokyo, Japan) for testing, and the PI fibrous membrane was dried at  $100^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $10\mathrm{h}$  before the test to remove the adsorbed moisture.
82: 
83: Thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) of PI NFMs was tested by a TA-Q50 thermal analysis system (Perkin-Elmer, Waltham, MA, USA) at a test temperature range of  $30 - 760^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  and a heating rate of  $20^{\circ}\mathrm{C / min}$ ; the test environment was nitrogen and the gas flow rate was  $20\mathrm{mL / min}$ . Differential scanning calorimetry (DSC) was tested with a DSC 214 calorimetric differential scanner (NETZSCH, Selb, Germany); the test temperature range was  $30 - 400^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , the heating rate was  $10^{\circ}\mathrm{C / min}$ , and the test environment was nitrogen with a gas flow of  $20\mathrm{mL / min}$ . The rheological evaluation was carried out on an AR2000 rheometer (TA Instrument, Newcastle, DE, USA). METI powder was pressed to obtain a sample disk (diameter:  $25\mathrm{mm}$ , thickness:  $1.5 \pm 0.2\mathrm{mm}$ ). During the measurement, METI discs were loaded into parallel plates. The top parallel plate oscillated at a fixed angular frequency of  $0.5\mathrm{Hz}$  and a fixed strain of  $1.0\%$ ; the data acquisition range was  $100 - 440^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  and the heating rate was  $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C / min}$ . The single-lap shear strength (LSS) test was performed on an Instron 5567 tensile machine (Instron, Boston, MA, USA). The elongation speed was  $2\mathrm{mm / min}$ .

85: # 2.3. Synthesis of METI Resins
86: 
87: Synthesis of soluble PI resin: METI resin was prepared by a high-temperature polycondensation method, using ODPA, BAPPT, and MEPA as raw materials, by controlling the ratio of the three raw materials. METI resins with molecular weights of  $5000\mathrm{g / mol}$ ,  $10,000\mathrm{g / mol}$ , and  $20,000\mathrm{g / mol}$  were designed and named METI-5K, 10K, and 20K, respectively. The following presentation takes METI-20K as an example to introduce the synthesis process of METI resin in detail: BAPPT (19.3218 g, 38.6019 mmol) and NMP (100 g) were charged into a  $500~\mathrm{mL}$  three-necked flask equipped with a mechanical stirrer, an electric heating bath, and a Dean-Stark trap and purged with nitrogen at room temperature, then ODPA (11.5094 g, 37.1019 mmol) was added to the solution and stirred for  $5\mathrm{h}$ . MEPA (0.5585 g, 3 mmol) and NMP (25 g) were added at the same time, and the solid content of the reaction mixture was controlled to be about  $20\mathrm{wt\%}$ . After stirring for another  $14\mathrm{h}$  at room temperature, toluene (100 g) and isoquinoline (1.0 g) were then added to the solution after stirring for a further  $14\mathrm{h}$  at room temperature. The reaction mixture was heated to  $140 - 145^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , and the by-product water generated by the reaction was removed by toluene/water azeotrope. The refluxing dehydration reaction was maintained for  $16\mathrm{h}$ . Then, toluene was distilled from the reaction system until the internal temperature of the reaction reached  $180^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , and the reaction was carried out at  $180^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\mathrm{h}$ . The solution was cooled to  $70^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  and the resulting viscous solution was precipitated in excess aqueous ethanol  $(10\mathrm{wt\%})$ . Finally, the precipitated METI-20K resin was dried at room temperature for  $24\mathrm{h}$ , and then dried under vacuum at  $130^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $24\mathrm{h}$ . Finally, white filamentous METI-20K resin was obtained, yield:  $29.62\mathrm{g}(97.7\%)$ .

89: The METI-10K and METI-5K resins were prepared according to a procedure similar to the one mentioned above, with the formula shown in Table 1. METI-5K: yield:  $28.76\mathrm{g}$ $(95.8\%)$ . METI-10K: yield:  $29.08\mathrm{g}$ $(96.9\%)$ .

97: # 2.4. Preparation of METI NFMs
98: 
99: Taking METI-20K NFM as an example, the detailed preparation process of METI NFMs is as follows: The completely dried METI-20K resin was dissolved in DMAc solution, and the solid content of the solution was controlled at  $33\mathrm{wt}\%$  to obtain a solution with an absolute viscosity of  $5000\mathrm{mPa}$  s. The obtained METI-20K solution was uniformly injected into a  $5\mathrm{mL}$  syringe using a spinneret with an inner diameter of  $0.21\mathrm{mm}$ . The METI-20K solution was extruded from the spinneret with a syringe pump at a rate of  $0.2\mathrm{mL/h}$ . The positive voltage between the injector and the collector was  $16\mathrm{kV}$  and the negative voltage was  $-3\mathrm{kV}$ . The distance between the spinneret and the grounded drum collector (diameter:  $10\mathrm{cm}$ ; length:  $30\mathrm{cm}$ ) was  $15\mathrm{cm}$ . The relative humidity of the electrospinning device was controlled at  $30\pm 5\%$ , and the speed of the collector was set at  $2\mathrm{rpm}$ . METI-20K fibers were densely wound and deposited on aluminum foil. The resulting METI-20K NFM was vacuum-dried at  $120^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\mathrm{h}$  to remove residual solvent.
100: 
101: METI-5K and METI-10K NFMs were prepared following the same procedure as above, but starting with either a METI-5K or METI-10K solution with a solid content of  $47.5\mathrm{wt}\%$  or  $38\mathrm{wt}\%$  (absolute viscosity:  $\sim 5000\mathrm{mPa}$  s), respectively, in the electrospinning solution.

103: # 2.5. Preparation of Stainless Steel Samples Adhered with the METI NFMs
104: 
105: The stainless steel adherends  $(100\mathrm{mm}\times 25.4\mathrm{mm}\times 2\mathrm{mm},$  length  $\times$  width  $\times$  height) used for the lap shear stress (LSS) test were pretreated by polishing with standard sandpaper before use. The METI UFMs were cut into small pieces  $(12.5\mathrm{mm}\times 25.4\mathrm{mm}$  length  $\times$  width), stacked, and placed between two stainless steel adherends. The thickness of the final cured METI NFMs was controlled to be  $0.3 - 0.5\mathrm{mm}$ . The overlapping area was  $12.5\mathrm{mm}\times 25.4\mathrm{mm}$  (length  $\times$  width). The METI NFMs were tightly clamped to the overlap of two stainless steel sheets. Subsequently, the METI NFM-adhered specimens were placed into an oven and then heated at  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ,  $150^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ,  $220^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ,  $290^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ,  $310^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , and  $330^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  each for  $1\mathrm{h}$ . Then, the samples were cooled to room temperature. The LSS values at room temperature  $(25^{\circ}\mathrm{C})$  were measured according to standard of GB/T 7124-2008 [30], "Determination of tensile shear strength of adhesives (rigid material vs. rigid material)". The high-temperature  $(200^{\circ}\mathrm{C})$  LSS tests were carried out according to the standard of GJB 444-1988 [31], "Test method for high temperature tensile shear strength of adhesives (metal to metal)". In each LSS test, five specimens were tested and the average data were recorded.
