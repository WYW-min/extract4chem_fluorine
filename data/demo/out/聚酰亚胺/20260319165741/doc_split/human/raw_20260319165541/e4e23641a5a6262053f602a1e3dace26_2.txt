56: # 2. Materials and Methods

58: # 2.1.Materials
59: 
60: Benzoin, sodium borohydride  $(\mathrm{NaBH}_4, \geq 98.0\%)$ , 4-nitrobenzoyl chloride (4-NBC,  $98\%$ ), methanol  $(\mathrm{MeOH}, \geq 99.8\%)$ , tetrahydrofuran  $(\mathrm{THF}, \geq 99.9\%)$ ,  $\mathrm{N,N'}$ -dimethylacetamide (DMAc,  $\geq 99.8\%$ ), pyromellitic dianhydride (PMDA), and  $4,4'$ -oxydianiline (ODA) were purchased from Sigma-Aldrich Korea (Seoul, Korea). Solvents such as N,N-dimethylformamide (DMF), dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO), N-methyl pyrrolidone (NMP), chloroform  $(\mathrm{CHCl}_3)$ , and ethanol were purchased from Junsei Chemical Co (Tokyo, Japan). As for 4,4-Diphthalic anhydride (6-FDA,  $\geq 98.0\%$  ) and  $10\%$  palladium on carbon  $(\mathrm{Pd} / \mathrm{C})$ , they were purchased from Tokyo Chemical Industry Co. (TCI) (Tokyo, Japan). n-Hexane, ethyl acetate, and dichloromethane were purchased from Samchun Pure Chemicals (Pyeongtaek, Korea). Celite 545, which is a kind of diatomite whose main component is silica and is used to separate a catalyst from the reaction mixture, and  $\gamma$ -butyrolactone were purchased from Daejung (Siheung, Korea). The deionized water used in the experiments was purified by a Direct- $\mathbf{Q}^{\text{®}}$ 3 water purification system (EMD Millipore) (Busan, Korea). All purchased chemical reagents were used without further purification.

62: # 2.2. Synthesis of 1,2-Diphenylethane-1,2-diol
63: 
64: 1,2-Diphenylethane-1,2-diol was synthesized according to the literature [36]. The synthesis of 1,2-Diphenylethane-1,2-diol was achieved by a reduction reaction using sodium borohydride as a catalyst. First,  $5.0\mathrm{g}$  of benzoin was added to a  $250~\mathrm{mL}$  round bottom flask. Then,  $80~\mathrm{mL}$  of methanol was added as a solvent. After  $30\mathrm{min}$  of stirring,  $1.3\mathrm{g}$  of sodium borohydride  $\mathrm{(NaBH_4)}$  was slowly added to the flask in three portions. After stirring for  $2\mathrm{h}$ ,  $50~\mathrm{mL}$  of deionized water was poured into the flask. The mixture was separated using a separation funnel. The organic layer was collected and dehydrated by magnesium sulfate. After that, the solution was concentrated through a rotary evaporator. The final powder was obtained after drying at  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  overnight (yield:  $95\%$ , m.p.:  $136.7^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ).
65: 
66: # 2.3. Synthesis of 1,2-Diphenylethane-1,2-diyl bis(4-Nitrobenzoate) (1,2-DPEDBN)
67: 
68: The synthesis of 1,2-Diphenylethane-1,2-diyl bis(4-nitrobenzoate) (1,2-DPEDBN) was achieved according to the literature [29] with slight modification (Scheme 1). The synthesis of 1,2-Diphenylethane-1,2-diyl bis(4-nitrobenzoate) was achieved by reacting with 4-nitrobenzoyl chloride (4-NBC) in the presence of pyridine as an acid acceptor. First,  $5.0\mathrm{g}$  of 1,2-diphenylethane-1,2-diol was added into a  $500~\mathrm{mL}$  round bottom flask sealed with a septum cap. Then,  $25~\mathrm{mL}$  of anhydrous tetrahydrofuran was added to the flask. The solution was stirred for  $30\mathrm{min}$  and cooled at  $0^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . After that,  $8.65\mathrm{g}$  of 4-nitrobenzoyl chloride was dissolved in  $25~\mathrm{mL}$  of THF in a separate round bottom flask under a nitrogen atmosphere. A 4-NBC solution was added dropwise with continuous stirring. Then,  $11.29~\mathrm{mL}$  of pyridine was slowly added into the flask. The mixture was stirred at room temperature for  $12\mathrm{h}$ . The precipitate was collected by vacuum filtration and washed with deionized water and n-hexane. The final product was obtained after drying at  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $15\mathrm{h}$  (yield:  $93\%$ , m.p.:  $235.1^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ).

73: # 2.4. Synthesis of 1,2-Diphenylethane-1,2-diyl bis(4-Aminobenzoate) (1,2-DPEDBA)
74: 
75: The overall design of the hydrogenation reaction refers to a previous work [37] (Scheme 1). The preparation of 1,2-Diphenylethane-1,2-diyl bis(4-aminobenzoate) was achieved by a hydrogenation reaction using palladium on carbon  $(\mathrm{Pd} / \mathrm{C})$  as a catalyst. First,  $5.0\mathrm{g}$  of 1,2-DPEDBN and  $0.5\mathrm{g}$  of palladium on carbon were added into a  $250~\mathrm{mL}$  round bottom flask under a nitrogen atmosphere. Then,  $120~\mathrm{mL}$  of anhydrous THF was added to the flask. After  $30\mathrm{min}$  of stirring, hydrogen balloons were placed into the flask. The reaction was continuously stirred for  $48\mathrm{h}$ . After that, the catalyst was filtered by Celite 525, and the filtrate was concentrated by a rotary evaporator. The final product was recrystallized by n-hexane and filtered. After vacuum drying at  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $15\mathrm{h}$ , 1,2-DPEDBA was obtained (yield:  $85.4\%$ , m.p.:  $248.8^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ ).

77: # 2.5. Preparation of 6FDA-DPEDBA PI and PMDA-ODA PI
78: 
79: As a precursor of PI, poly(amic acid) (PAA) was prepared by polycondensation polymerization with diamine and dianhydride. A solution of PAA with a 25 wt. % of solid content was prepared by a condensation reaction between 2.0 mmol of 1,2-DPEDBA (933 mg) and 2.0 mmol of 6-FDA under a nitrogen atmosphere. The solution was stirred for 24 h at room temperature. After that, 0.81 mL of pyridine and 1.89 mL of acetic anhydride were added dropwise into the solution. Under a nitrogen atmosphere, the solution was stirred for 24 h. After stirring was finished, the solution was slowly poured into 200 mL of methanol. The precipitate was filtered and washed with deionized water and methanol. The final product was obtained after drying at 80 °C for 24 h.
80: 
81: The 6FDA-DPEDBA film was prepared by dissolving  $0.35\mathrm{g}$  of the PI powder into  $2.78~\mathrm{mL}$  of  $\gamma$ -butyrolactone (solid content of  $10\mathrm{wt.\%}$ ). The solution was stirred until the solution became transparent and homogeneous. After that, the solution was cast onto a glass substrate and dried under a nitrogen atmosphere at  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $24\mathrm{h}$ . Scheme 1 illustrates the synthetic route for 6FDA-DPEDBA PI as well as 1,2-DPEDBN and 1,2-DPEDBA.
82: 
83: For comparison, poly(pyromellitic dianhydride-co-4,4'-oxydianiline) (PMDA-ODA PI) was also synthesized. The PMDA-ODA PI synthetic route followed the literature [38]. A solution of PAA was prepared by polymerization and thermal imidization of diamine (ODA) and dianhydride (PMDA). First, equimolar amounts of ODA  $(2.0\mathrm{mmol})$  and PMDA  $(2.0\mathrm{mmol})$  were placed into  $7.1\mathrm{mL}$  of DMAc. After stirring for  $24\mathrm{h}$  under nitrogen at room temperature, PAA was poured onto a glass slide using a dropper and dried at  $60^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  under a nitrogen atmosphere overnight. Next, the film was thermally imidized by the following process:  $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $2\mathrm{h}$ ,  $120^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\mathrm{h}$ ,  $180^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\mathrm{h}$ ,  $250^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $0.5\mathrm{h}$ , and  $300^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $0.5\mathrm{h}$  under a heating rate of  $1^{\circ}\mathrm{C / min}$ . The final film was obtained by immersing the slide glass in deionized water.

85: # 2.6. Measurements and Characterization
86: 
87: # 2.6.1. Melting Point
88: 
89: The melting point (m.p.) of each organic compound was measured by differential scanning calorimeter (DSC) using Discovery DSC 25 (TA instruments) (New Castle, DE, USA) at a heating rate of  $10^{\circ}\mathrm{C / min}$  in the temperature range of  $30 - 250^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ .
90: 
91: # 2.6.2. Molecular Weight
92: 
93: The number-average molecular weight  $(\mathrm{M_n})$  and weight-average molecular weight  $(\mathrm{M_w})$  were analyzed using polystyrene standards by gel permeation chromatography (GPC) using a Waters 1515-2414 GPC (Milford, MA, USA) at  $35^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . Tetrahydrofuran (THF) was used as a solvent.
94: 
95: # 2.6.3. X-ray Diffraction
96: 
97: Wide-angle X-ray diffraction (XRD, Bruker AXS) (Billerica, MA, USA) was conducted using Cu K $\alpha$  radiation ( $\lambda = 1.5418 \AA$ ) at  $40 \mathrm{kV}$  and  $40 \mathrm{~mA}$  at  $2\theta$  values of  $5 \sim 80^{\circ}$ .
98: 
99: # 2.6.4. Thermogravimetric Analysis
100: 
101: The thermal stability of the PI film was characterized by thermogravimetric analysis (TGA, TGA Q500, TA instruments) (New Castle, DE, USA) at  $30 - 800^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  with a heating rate of  $10^{\circ}\mathrm{C / min}$  under a nitrogen atmosphere.
102: 
103: # 2.6.5. Water Absorption and Water Contact Angle
104: 
105: The water absorption behavior was tested by weighing the vacuum-dried PI film and soaking it in deionized water at room temperature for  $24\mathrm{h}$  and calculated using Equation (1):
106: 
107: $$
108: W _ {A} = \left[ \frac {W - W _ {0}}{W _ {0}} \right] \times 1 0 0 \tag {1}
109: $$
110: 
111: where  $W_{0}$  is the vacuum-dried PI film weight and  $W$  is the weight of the PI film wetted in deionized water for  $24\mathrm{h}$ . The water contact angle of the PI film was analyzed by a contact angle analyzer (SEO Pheonix 300) (Suwon, Korea) according to the sessile drop method at room temperature using a  $3\mathrm{mL}$  syringe with a needle of  $0.127\mathrm{mm}$  in diameter. The water volume used in each measurement was  $30~\mu \mathrm{L}$ . Five readings of both static contact angles for each sample were taken and averaged to obtain a reliable value.
112: 
113: # 2.6.6. Dielectric Constant
114: 
115: The dielectric properties of the PI film were analyzed by a broad frequency dielectric spectrometer (Concept 80, Novocontrol) (Montabaur, Germany) in the range of  $10^{0} - 10^{7}\mathrm{Hz}$  at  $25^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ .
116: 
117: # 2.6.7. FTIR Spectroscopy
118: 
119: The functional groups of the synthesized monomers and the novel PI film were characterized by Fourier transform infrared (FT-IR) spectroscopy using an FTIR-4100 (JASCO Co.) (Tokyo, Japan) in the range of  $500 - 4000\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$  with a resolution of  $2\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$ .
120: 
121: # 2.6.8. NMR Spectroscopy
122: 
123: The synthesized materials were also characterized by  ${}^{1}\mathrm{H}$ -nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) and  ${}^{13}\mathrm{C}$ -NMR spectroscopy (Varian MR-400 spectrometer, Agilent Technologies) (Palo Alto, CA, USA) at a frequency of  $400\mathrm{MHz}$  using deuterated dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO- $\mathrm{d}_6$ ) as the NMR solvent.
124: 
125: # 2.6.9. UV-Visible Spectroscopy
126: 
127: UV-visible optical transmission spectroscopy (UV-1650, Shimadzu) (Kyoto, Japan) was performed on the PI films in the wavelength range of  $200 - 800\mathrm{nm}$  with a resolution of  $0.5\mathrm{nm}$  and a scanning rate of  $300\mathrm{nm}$  per  $\mathrm{min}^{-1}$ .
