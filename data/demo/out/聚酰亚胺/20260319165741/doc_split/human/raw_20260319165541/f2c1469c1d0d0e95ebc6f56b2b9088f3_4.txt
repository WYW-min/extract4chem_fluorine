From a structural point of view, BPDA-PDA and BPDA-BZ had rodlike phenyl structures, BPDA-ODA had hinged polar ether groups  $(\mathrm{O}-)$ , and BPDA-DDS had hinged big polar sulfonyl groups  $(\mathrm{SO}_2-$ . The rodlike phenyl structures of BPDA-PDA and BPDA-BZ were linearly connected without bent structures and a conformational change, and so this could lead to large chain orientation along the polymer chain. In contrast, ether groups of BPDA-ODA and sulfonyl groups of BPDA-DDS had bent structures, and this imparted flexibility to the polyimide chain, leading to changes in their morphology.

88: It is well known that the hardness of a polymer is largely determined by its chemical and morphological structures.[24] Generally, when a repeating unit of a polymer has a rodlike structure, the
89: 
90: toughness and strength should be high because such a structure accompanies large chain orientation along the polymer chain. In contrast, when a repeating unit has a bent or hinged structure, the toughness and strength are expected to be low. The bent linkages may weaken the intermolecular interactions among polymer chains (van der Waals interaction) and prevent effective molecular packing.

This reveals that there was no change in the crystallinity between the surface and the bulk of the film and that there was uniformity in the orientation of the molecules in the surface and the bulk. In BPDA-BZ, most of the molecules were oriented in the same plane, that is, in the direction of the film plane (in-plane orientation). However, the hardness value of the BPDA-BZ was lower than that of BPDA-PDA, indicating that BPDA-PDA had the better crystalline surface of the two. However, in BPDA-PDA, the hardness value decreased with an increase in the penetration depth, indicating that there was some out-of-plane orientation in the film. Hence, there was a difference in the orientation between the surface and bulk resulting in a decrease in the hardness value. However, in BPDA-BZ, all the molecules were preferentially oriented in the film plane, rather than out of plane, both in the surface and in the bulk, and so there was no change in the hardness value with an increase in the penetration depth. This is also supported by the birefringence results.
