39: Abstract: In this study, based on the preparation of hexazatriphenylene-ternary-anhydride (HAT-T), polyimide membranes were prepared by reaction of  $4,4^{\prime}$  (hexafluoroisopropylidene)diphthalic anhydride (6FDA),  $4,4^{\prime}$ -diaminodiphenyl sulfide (SDA),  $2,2^{\prime}$ -bis (trifluoromethyl)diaminobiphenyl (TFDB) and 5-amino-2-(4-aminophenyl) benzimidazole (PABZ). Polyimide films with a hexazobenzo structure have good film-forming properties, high molecular weight ( $M_{\mathrm{n}} = 0.79 - 11.79 \times 10^{6}$ ,  $M_{\mathrm{w}} = 1.03 - 16.60 \times 10^{6}$ ) and narrow molecular weight distribution (polymer dispersity index = 1.17-1.54). With the introduction of rigid HAT-T, the tensile strength and elongation at break of polyimide films are 195.63-510.37 MPa and  $4.00 - 9.70\%$ , respectively, with excellent mechanical properties. The gas separation performance test shows that hexazatriphenylene-containing polyimide films have good gas selectivity for  $\mathrm{CO}_{2} / \mathrm{CH}_{4}$ . In particular, the separation performance of PIc-t (6FDA/PABZ/HAT-T) surpasses the "2008 Robeson Upper Bound". The selectivity of 188.43 for  $\mathrm{CO}_{2} / \mathrm{CH}_{4}$  gas reveals its potential value in the separation and purification of methane gas.
