37: Abstract: Polymeric nanofibrous membranes (NFMs) with both high whiteness and high thermal and ultraviolet (UV) stability are highly desired as reflectors for ultraviolet light-emitting diodes (UV-LEDs) devices. In the current work, a semi-alicylic and fluoro-containing polyimide (PI) NFM with potential application in such kinds of circumstances was successfully fabricated from the organosoluble PI resin solution via a one-step electrospinning procedure. In order to achieve the target, a semi-alicylic PI resin was first designed and synthesized from an allyclic dianhydride, 3,4-dicarboxy1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8-decahydro-1-naphthalenesuccinic dianhydride (or hydrogenated tetralin dianhydride, HTDA), and a fluoro-containing diamine, 2,2-bis[4-(4-amino-phenoxy)phenyl]hexafluoropropane (BDAF), via an imidization procedure. The derived PI (HTDA-BDAF) resin possessed a number-average molecular weight  $(M_{\mathrm{n}})$  higher than  $33,000~\mathrm{g / mol}$  and was highly soluble in polar aprotic solvents, such as  $N,N$  -dimethylacetamide (DMAc). The electrospinning solution was prepared by dissolving the PI resin in DMAc at a solid content of 25-35 wt%. For comparison, the conventional high-whiteness polystyrene (PS) NFM was prepared according to a similar electrospinning procedure. The thermal and UV stability of the derived PI and PS NFMs were investigated by exposure under the UV-LED (wavelength:  $365~\mathrm{nm}$ ) irradiation. Various thermal evaluation results indicated that the developed PI (HTDA-BDAF) NFM could maintain both the high reflectance and high whiteness at elevated temperatures. For example, after thermal treatment at  $200^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\textrm{h}$  in air, the PI (HTDA-BDAF) NFM exhibited a reflectance at a wavelength of  $457~\mathrm{nm}$ $(R_{457})$  of  $89.0\%$  , which was comparable to that of the pristine PI NMF  $(R_{457} = 90.2\%)$ . The PI (HTDA-BDAF) NFM exhibited a whiteness index  $(WI)$  of 90.88, which was also close to that of the pristine sample  $(WI = 91.22)$ . However, for the PS NFM counterpart, the  $R_{457}$  value decreased from the pristine  $88.4\%$  to  $18.1\%$  after thermal treatment at  $150^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\textrm{h}$ , and the sample became transparent. The PI NFM maintained good optical and mechanical properties during the high dose  $(2670~\mathrm{J / cm}^2)$  of UV exposure, while the properties of the PS NFM apparently deteriorated under the same UV aging.
