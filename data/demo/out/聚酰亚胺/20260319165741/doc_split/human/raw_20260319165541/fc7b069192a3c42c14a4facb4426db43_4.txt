219: # 4. Conclusions
220: 
221: Semi-alicyclic and fluoro-containing PI NFM was designed and prepared in order to meet the requirements of high-power UV-LEDs. The synergistic effects of the alicyclic and fluoro-containing groups endowed the derived PI-3 (HTDA-BDAF) NFM with high whiteness  $(WI = 91.22$  at room temperature;  $WI = 90.88$  after thermal treatment at  $200^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\mathrm{h}$  in air), high UV reflectance  $(R_{457} = 90.2\%)$  at room temperature;  $R_{457} = 89.0\%$  after thermal treatment at  $200^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\mathrm{h}$  in air), good thermal stability  $(T_{\mathrm{g}} = 239.6^{\circ}\mathrm{C})$ , and good UV exposure stability. Comparatively, the standard high-whiteness PS NFM analogs lost the fiber structure only after thermal treatment at  $150^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$  for  $1\mathrm{h}$ . The good comprehensive properties of the currently developed PI NFMs are quite beneficial for their applications as reflectors or other optical components for high-power UV-LED devices.
